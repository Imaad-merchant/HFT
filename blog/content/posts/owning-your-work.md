Every platform gives you reach in exchange for dependence. That trade is usually worth it — until, quietly, it isn't.

The test I keep coming back to: if this platform disappeared tomorrow, what do I walk away with?

## The cheap version of independence

You don't need much. A domain. A folder of markdown files. A boring deploy pipeline. I keep trying to over-engineer this and it keeps punishing me. The less clever the setup, the more I actually write.

## What "owning" really means

Owning your work isn't only about the files. It's about the URL people bookmark, the feed they subscribe to, the name at the top of the page. Any one of those you hand to someone else is a door they hold the key to.

Nothing wrong with renting a door. Just know which ones you're renting.

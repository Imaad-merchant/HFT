There's a stretch of time between being accepted somewhere and showing up that nobody warns you about. You are already the thing, socially, before you are the thing in practice. Your inbox starts addressing you differently. People introduce you with the new noun.

I've been thinking about this as a kind of rehearsal — the way actors walk a stage with a script in hand before they know the lines.

## Rehearsals for the self

Most of adult life is rehearsing for a role that hasn't quite started. You buy the shoes for the job before the first day. You practice a signature for the title you don't yet hold. You imagine your students into being and then stand up straighter.

The trouble with rehearsals is that you can get stuck in them. You can spend a year performing the version of yourself that is about to arrive, and when she finally arrives she finds you exhausted.

## What helps

A few things, for me:

- Writing it down, in public, so the rehearsal becomes a draft instead of a loop.
- Keeping one place (a notebook, a site like this) where I am allowed to still be figuring it out.
- Remembering that my students will also be rehearsing, and that we are, in fact, rehearsing the same play together.

I got into grad school. That part is wonderful. The part that snuck up on me is that I'll have students in the fall, and they will, of course, google me.

It's not that I'm ashamed of anything on my personal feed. It's that the me who writes late at night, who posts half-thoughts at red lights, who is still figuring out what she believes — that me doesn't need to be the first thing a first-year sees before my first lecture.

So I'm splitting the account. One quiet, one loud. One for family and friends, one for whoever wants to come along. This site is the loud one's home base.

## Why a site and not just another handle

Because I'd like to own my work. Substack has been kind to me, but it is still renting. A domain and a few files in a folder are the smallest, most boring moat I can give myself, and they belong to me the way a notebook belongs to its owner.

## What to expect here

Essays. Articles grouped by topic so you can find the ones you actually want. A portfolio page that is mostly aspirational right now. A writings page for the things that aren't essays — letters, fragments, poems. And, eventually, a paid tier for the people who want to keep this going.

That's it. Welcome.
